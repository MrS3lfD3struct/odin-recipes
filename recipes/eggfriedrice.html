Pancake Recipe

Egg fried rice is a popular, simple, and fast Asian dish made by stir-frying cooked rice—ideally leftover or cold—with scrambled eggs, soy sauce, garlic, and scallions. It is a staple of Chinese cuisine, often served as a side or main dish, with countless variations including vegetables or sesame oil for extra flavor.

Ingredients

  • Rice: 2 to 3 cups cooked, cooled leftover white rice.
  • Aromatics: 1 tbsp chopped garlic, 3-4 scallions.
  • Seasoning: 2 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tsp sesame oil, salt, and white/black pepper to taste.
  • Veggies: 1/2 cup diced carrots, peas, or cabbage.
  • Oil: 2 tbsp high-smoke point oil

Preparation Steps

  1. Prep: If using fresh rice, spread it on a tray to cool completely to avoid mushy rice. Scramble eggs in a hot wok/pan until just set, then remove to a plate.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: Heat 1-2 tbsp oil over high heat. Add minced garlic and scallion whites (and optional veggies like carrots) and stir-fry for 30-60 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Fry Rice: Add the cooked, cold rice to the pan. Stir-fry rapidly for 2-3 minutes to break up clumps and heat through.
  4. Season: Pour soy sauce around the edges of the wok for a "seared" flavor. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Combine: Return the scrambled eggs to the wok. Mix everything together well, allowing the eggs to break into smaller pieces. Add scallion greens.
  6. Serve: Toss in sesame oil if desired and serve hot immediately.
