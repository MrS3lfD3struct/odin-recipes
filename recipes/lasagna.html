Lasagna Recipe

Lasagna is a traditional Italian baked dish featuring layers of wide, flat pasta alternating with fillings like ragù (meat sauce), béchamel or ricotta cheese, and mozzarella. It is a popular comfort food casserole, often served as a main course with garlic bread or salad. The dish is typically baked until bubbly and browned.

Ingredients

  • Pasta: 1 lb lasagna noodles (boiled or no-boil).
  • Meat Sauce: 1 lb ground beef or Italian sausage, onion, garlic, 24-32 oz marinara/pasta sauce.
  • Cheese Mixture: 15 oz ricotta cheese (or cottage cheese), large egg, 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese, 2 tbsp parsley.
  • Topping: 3-4 cups shredded mozzarella cheese.

Preparation Steps

  1. Prepare the Sauce: Brown the beef/sausage with onions and garlic, drain fat, and simmer with marinara sauce for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Make the Cheese Filling: Combine ricotta, egg, Parmesan, parsley, and salt/pepper in a bowl.
  3. Assemble: Spread a small amount of sauce on the bottom of a 9x13-inch pan, add a layer of noodles, then cheese mixture, then mozzarella. Repeat layers (typically 3 times).
  4. Bake: Cover with foil (tenting it to avoid sticking to cheese) and bake at 190 degree celsius for 30 minutes. Remove foil and bake 10-20 minutes more until bubbly and browned.
  5. Let the lasagna rest for 15-30 minutes before cutting to ensure clean slices.
